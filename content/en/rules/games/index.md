## Solo Play

The game was rather created to be played solo. You are Destiny and you explore Glorantha through characters and situations.

You are Destiny:

- Spider-Rock could be: the near-divine Uz priestess who knows the secrets of Arachne Solara?
- Or perhaps the eye of the Dragon sleeping beneath the mountain?
- The ghost of an Ambiguous Scholar who continues their study and experiments again and again, for eternity or until the End of Time?
- The spirit of a forgotten deity in search of understanding about the reason for their exile?
- ...

You watch Glorantha live and die.

You are the hands that weigh the Runes.

You are the voice that tells the story.

You choose where to go and when and what to look at.

You choose the themes that are dear to you.

The place, the era, the characters.

Do not limit yourself.

You want to enter the bedchamber of the Son of the Moon, go ahead.

You want to try to change the world in a Heroquest, go ahead.

You want to visit the market of Nochet, go ahead.

You want to see the world through the eyes of a trollkin or a broo, go ahead.

### In Practice

- I choose a **setting** I want to explore
- I then create the **protagonists** (briefly or in detail depending on my solo game project: short or long)
- I use the **runes** of power whenever I need to answer a question: who, what, why, how, etc... or I translate the uncertainty into a cosmic conflict between one or more poles (1 vs 1 most of the time)
- I **research** the part of the universe we are in to see if there are must-explore elements that would highlight the world
- I then imagine a seed of a **situation** into which I plunge the protagonists
- I imagine the **interaction** and their **objectives**
- If there is uncertainty in the choices, I proceed to **oppositions**
- I use the [**Destiny gauge**](../stakes/index.md#ou-bien-utiliser-une-jauge-pour-le-destin)
- Destiny also allows me to negotiate certain gains and losses in a balanced way. When outside of a conflict, I favor the protagonists, I favor adversity at the level of destiny and vice versa.
- For me a situation must lead to at least one **conflict** for it to be interesting to play
- I try to play the **scene** through to the end
- And at the end, I even roll under the Destiny value (the number of dice or the absolute value of Destiny): if the result is strictly lower, then a **plot twist** occurs.
- The scene ends and I can naturally move on to the next situation

## Group Play

Although designed for solo mode, it should be possible to play in a group with players who know Glorantha well or a GM who does the necessary teaching work to convey to the PCs what is needed to propose stakes coherent with Glorantha.

We will see further on that the game mechanic invites the player to embrace a frame of thought specific to Glorantha.

### Classic RPG Mode

One of the players will play Destiny (the GM) and the players will each have a character to play.

The GM can run a pre-designed scenario but also improvise from rune draws.

The GM has the final say on the stakes.

### Shared Mode

All players play Destiny. We take turns. We start with a player who presents their idea and draws the runes. We begin the story. When there are multiple characters in a scene, we distribute roles among players to determine the stakes and play the characters.

We change Destiny at the end of each scene (clockwise, for example).

The group could even function with a mix of players who know the lore well and players discovering the world.
