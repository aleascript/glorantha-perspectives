---
title: "Illumination"
---

We know that it can be received through a question-and-answer duel.

It is actually a special case of an opposition with a mystical party.

And Illumination can naturally emerge in very specific dice configurations.

The underlying idea is that the other side understands (or even starts playing the 1s in a mystical way) to win the victory or reach a tie. The configurations are therefore broader than the two textbook cases presented just below.

Possible examples:

### The Enlightening Stupor

> The opposing camp makes a draw that should have crushed the mystic, but the mystical rules end up completely reversing the result. The shock felt can generate an Illumination.

- Camps containing the same number of stakes
- Mystical side: almost all 1s and one 6
- Other side: as many 6s as the mystical camp has 1s, and one odd 1
- Result: mystical camp: 6 and other camp: 1

### The Revelation through the Tilt

> The opposing camp finds itself in a position to play a mystical move to change the final result. If they do so, they approach or embrace Illumination.

- Other side, draw: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 (21) (III, IV or II)
- Mystical side: 6, 1, 6, 1, 6, 1 (21) (III)
- Result:
    - Other: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 (15) (II or III)
    - Mystical: 6, 1, 6, 1, 6 (20) (III)
- But the other can understand and play mystically (their 1) and then:
    - Other: 2, 3, 4, 5 (14) (II)
    - Mystical: 1, 6, 1, 6 (14) (II)
- Tie rather than failure

This configuration is of course ideal. It is a textbook case told in Lunar universities that shows the proportional exchanges at work in the cosmos and the possibility of balance if one accepts to change perspective.

> Note: before Illumination, Lunarians speak of the Spark. It is a way of perceiving Illumination, of glimpsing it without it being fully integrated and radically changing the frame of thought of the one experiencing it.
