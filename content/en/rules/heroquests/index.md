---
title: "Heroquests"
---

Since HeroWars we know that one must:

- Access the Other Side
- Realize the steps of a myth
- This can be the possibility, following the success of one's entry into the Other Side, of accessing the heroic mode of resolution for one's frame of thought (with entities facing you who also have this mode)

The system allows determining the results of each step. You can therefore narratively determine a level of victory for the quest. And the narration should especially serve to describe the effects on the real world.

Indeed, depending on the level of victory, and depending on the acts performed during the quest, consequences flow for the heroes and the world. All of this sometimes in parallel.

Some aspects of heroquests:

- Changes are lasting
- There are surprises
- You often stake an aptitude before starting
- There is synchronicity between the divine world and the real world (in both directions).
- Heroes benefit from following the path of the myth. But it is possible to deviate from it at one's own risk and peril.
- Myths shape reality. The path of the myth explains and confirms why real things happen as they do. Therefore, if one deviates from the path, one can change the reality of the real world!
- More pronounced [runic colors](../../notes/runic-colors)
- A [Otherworld marked by runes](../../notes/runic-forms/)

Even though Gloranthian myths are already very numerous to explore, you may want to [invent](myths) some.

You could even return from a Heroquest [deeply transformed](../../notes/new-referentials).
