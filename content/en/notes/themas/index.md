---
title: "Gloranthan Themes"
---

Some themes from the HeroWars but also rooted in other eras.

Some themes are strongly culturally typed (Orlanthi, mystic, West, shamans, …)

- **Rebirth(s) of the hero.** The Hero undergoes a cycle of transformation in the manner of Arkat/Gbaji.
  
- **Man and myth.** The Hero must find their place among the Gods: conform to their essence, negotiate with them, go against them, replay the myths as they are or modify them... Such are the possibilities that open up.
  
- **I fought, We won.** This Orlanthi motto designates the freedom to choose one's enemies and to fight them. This individual struggle is ultimately a liberation for all. Just as when Orlanth killed Yelm to free the gods, which led to the Great Compromise.
  
- The HeroWars oppose **what was and what will be**: the cycles of destruction and rebirth have been traversed once again. The actions of your Heroes decide the future of the world. Will they preserve the ancient rules or create a new world on the ruins of the old?
  
- The HeroWars oppose **the Storm and the Sky**. Since the very beginning of myths and memories, war has raged between Orlanth, the Eternal Rebel, and Yelm, the Infinite Emperor. Above the world, storm clouds boil, engaged in an eternal struggle against the power of the burning arrows of the Sun. They fought for power in the past, and their worshipers say it is time to bring the struggle to its end.
  
- The HeroWars oppose **Tradition and Progress**. Upstarts and opportunists come to challenge the old ways of life that have proven themselves by maintaining a certain peace. The elders and those who support traditions are outraged and see a social explosion. The young and the creative consider themselves innovators and saviors.

- The HeroWars oppose **Life and Death**. Life and Death are both part of the world. Plants, animals, people, and even the world itself — everything lives, dies, and is reborn. So goes existence. But some seek to interrupt this cycle so that everything that dies remains dead. The defenders of Life are beginning to awaken to resist these destroyers, and they will not let them break their world.

- The HeroWars oppose **the Cosmos and Chaos**. The gods are the guardians of the Eternal Order called the Cosmos. Since creation, the Cosmos has been confronted by Chaos, which gathers everything that cannot adapt to the Cosmos or cannot exist within it. Since creation, Chaos has tried to conquer, corrupt, or destroy the Cosmos, and the very essence of the gods is to resist this power.
  
- The HeroWars oppose **Light and Darkness**. This conflict, several ages old, has never ended. It resurfaces today in Glorantha, igniting and fading with the rhythm of the eternal energies whose flow imbues the mortal world with power.
  
- The HeroWars oppose **Orlanth and the Red Goddess**. Orlanth is change, the free winds that blow across the worlds. He has always led his people toward greatness, but today he is trapped. The Red Goddess seeks to smother his freedom so that the entire world knows her deceptive peace. Orlanth resists the malevolent Shepherd and his worshipers struggle to ensure that their great god can remain free and powerful.

- The HeroWars oppose **the Inclusive Empire and tribal cultures**. The Empire of Sedenya seeks to impose total peace by all means, absorbing the ways of life of its enemies when they are compatible. All resistance is futile in its eyes. Many fight to prevent their culture from being disintegrated or transformed to the point of losing its identity.
  
- The HeroWars oppose **Law and War**. The Laws of the Invisible God define the world and set the rules by which people must live. But the Kingdom of War knows no law. Its insatiable appetite for death and destruction threatens to drag everything into oblivion. All of Fronela has gathered to fight it, under the direction of the order of the Swallow, which must prove that law is more powerful than war.

- The HeroWars oppose **the World and the Otherworld**. The Otherworld is normally separated from the mortal world and is perceived by only a handful of powerful people. But the barriers between humans and spirits seem to be collapsing. The traditions that have always protected people against evil spirits no longer work as well as before, or the malevolent forces that allow evil spirits to come into our world have become more powerful. When the walls between worlds disappear, there will be no place for ordinary people. Only the bravest men and the most gifted magicians will be able to prevent evil from crossing the barrier.
  
- The HeroWars oppose **Liberation and Temptation**. Humanity has always been torn between the destructive temptations of the world and the liberation obtained by transcending the illusion of existence. For every great master who taught a path of liberation, a deceptive leader led men even deeper into the nets of the world. This struggle will always endure because such is the nature of humans: to seek perfection but stop at pleasure.

- The HeroWars oppose **Magic and Nature**. Several times in history, the actions of mortals have had profound consequences on the world. In the First Age, the great acts of high magic by several peoples caused the Sun to stop in the sky. In the Second Age, simultaneous curses closed the seas to all navigation and destroyed several countries with the Greeting. In the Third Age, the long-term plans of a dead goddess caused the birth of Sedenya and the rise of the Red Moon. Many races and cultures are at the heart of rituals several centuries old, and the result they pursue — whether it be to cause a flood or reforestation, to rebuild or devour, to raise or tear down — will be decisive in the course of the HeroWars and will once again shape Glorantha.

- The HeroWars oppose **free will and dictatorship**. The intelligent beings of Glorantha follow the orders of gods, spirits, or saints; however, they need to express themselves without the constraints of the Other Side. Most people continue to obey their Otherworldly masters, but some steadfast souls break free and follow paths full of freedom and the unknown.

- The HeroWars oppose **beings of the physical world and the Otherworld**. The deities of the Other Side — whether gods, spirits, or saints — have their own objectives and their own plans, and they expect mortals to carry them out. Most mortals have no say in their destiny, but heroes can resist the machinations of Otherworldly deities and choose their own future, even traveling to the Other Side to confront its inhabitants directly.

- The HeroWars oppose **our customs and their customs**. Every person in Glorantha needs companions, protection, and support. Every culture considers its way of life to be the best, and each resists the efforts of others to impose foreign customs. A conflict of this type takes place within the microcosm of the Dragon Pass, where the Heortlians resist the seductive methods of the Lunars.

- The HeroWars oppose **Us and Them**. You can trust us, the members of your group, but can you trust someone who doesn't follow our rules, worships a different god, and doesn't speak our language? Even if they have the same goals as us, how can we be certain they don't wish us harm?

- The HeroWars oppose **Humans and the Elder Races**. There was a time when humans were just one people among many, weaker even than most other peoples. But the Gods' War struck the Elder Races hard. Since the Dawn, their numbers and power have declined, and they have been pushed toward isolated lands while human civilizations expanded. Despite significant successes throughout history, most humans believe the Elder Races are weak and will eventually die out. They are wrong. The Elder Races existed before humans were even imagined, and their plots are just as old. The Aldryami, the Mostali, the Uz, the Oceanides, and the lesser races have gathered their strength for centuries, and they are ready to unleash their vengeance on unsuspecting humanity. Soon, the kingdoms of the Elder Races will expand once again, and it will be humans who are pushed back and exterminated.

- The world is complex and open enough to address **any theme** you might find interesting to develop... **YGWV**
